# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We expect all members of the flask-validate community to treat each other with respect and to maintain a harassment-free environment.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Be respectful** – Treat all community members with kindness and respect, regardless of their background, experience level, or opinions
- **Be inclusive** – Welcome and support people of all backgrounds and identities
- **Be constructive** – Provide feedback that is helpful, specific, and intended to improve
- **Listen actively** – Make an effort to understand different perspectives and opinions
- **Be professional** – Maintain a professional tone in all interactions
- **Give credit** – Acknowledge the contributions of others appropriately

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable and will not be tolerated:

- **Harassment** – Unwelcome comments, images, or behavior based on protected characteristics (race, color, religion, gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, or other protected statuses)
- **Intimidation** – Deliberately intimidating or threatening behavior
- **Discrimination** – Treating individuals differently based on any protected characteristic or personal attribute
- **Offensive language** – Using slurs, derogatory language, or offensive jokes
- **Trolling** – Deliberately posting inflammatory, off-topic, or disruptive messages
- **Doxing** – Sharing private information without consent
- **Unwelcome sexual advances** – Any unwelcome sexual comments, imagery, or behavior
- **Abuse** – Personal attacks, insults, or aggressive behavior

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All spaces managed by the flask-validate project, including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and documentation
- Official project events, whether online or in-person
- Informal gatherings and social events organized by project members in their official capacity
- Any communication that references the project or community

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness a violation of this Code of Conduct, please report it by:

1. **Direct communication** – Contact a project maintainer directly with details of the incident
2. **Email** – Send a detailed report to the project maintainers (details to be provided)
3. **Anonymous reporting** – Use the project's anonymous reporting mechanism (if available)

Please include:
- A description of what happened
- Who was involved
- When it occurred
- Any relevant context or evidence

### Response

Project maintainers will:

- Acknowledge receipt of your report within 48 hours
- Investigate the matter promptly and confidentially
- Take appropriate action based on the findings
- Keep you informed of the outcome (where appropriate and possible)

### Consequences

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

1. **Warning** – A private warning and discussion about the behavior
2. **Temporary suspension** – Temporary restriction from project spaces and participation
3. **Permanent removal** – Permanent ban from the project and community spaces

The severity of consequences depends on:
- The nature and severity of the violation
- Whether this is a first offense or repeated behavior
- The impact on community members
- Whether the person shows genuine remorse and commitment to change

## Appeals

If you believe a decision was made in error, you may appeal to the project maintainers with:
- A clear explanation of why you believe the decision was unfair
- Any additional context or evidence
- A commitment to future compliance with this Code of Conduct

Appeals will be reviewed fairly and impartially.
